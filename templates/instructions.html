Northeastern University, Khoury College of Computer and Information Science

Names of Investigators: Cody Dunne, Wolfgang Gatterbauer, Mirek Riedewald, Aristotelis Sigiouan Leventidis

Title of Project: Visualizing Database Queries

Request to participate in usability testing: We would like to invite you to participate in a web-based online usability test.

Qualifications:We are asking you to participate in this test because you are experienced with using SQL, are at least 18 years old and you are located in the USA.

Key Information

  • Your consent is being sought for participation in a usability test and your participation is voluntary.
  • The purpose of this test is to compare the effectiveness of a visual metaphor in helping users understand SQL vs. alternatives (e.g., formatted SQL text).
  • Time required:This test is expected to take about 30 minutes to complete (including qualification test) with a maximum limit of 65 minutes (15 minutes for the qualification and 50 minutes for the main test) and no limit for the tutorial and questionnaire except the HIT expiration time of 120 minutes. We have also included an embedded countdown timer to display the amount of time remaining. The timer can be found at the top left of the test window once the test starts. We highly recommend that you take the test in one sitting with your browser window maximized for better readability.
  • The procedures that you will be asked to complete include a pre-test qualification test, reading through a tutorial, answering questions about the plain-English results of SQL queries and visual representations, and answering a simple questionnaire about SQL experience and demographics. You need to answer at least 4/6 questions correctly in our SQL comprehension test in order to qualify for the hit.
  • There are no foreseeable risks or discomforts to you for taking part in this test.
  • There are not direct benefits to you participating in the test.

Participation: The decision to participate in this usability test is voluntary. You do not have to participate and you can refuse to answer any question. Even if you begin the web-based online test, you can stop at any time. There are no foreseeable risks or discomforts to you for taking part in this test.

HIT acceptance criteria: You need to answer at least 5 out of the 12 questions correctly Your evaluation is done automatically, but your HIT acceptance and bonus assignment may take up to 48 hours to process.

Payment: You will be paid a base pay of $5.20 for correctly answering 5/12 questions. You will receive a bonus $1.04 for each correct answer after the first 5 for a total pay of $12.48. In addition to correctness, you will also receive a bonus if you complete the test within a certain time limit. The bonus is applied as a percentage to your total pay. The detailed time brackets with their respective bonus percentages are shown by the table below:

Total Time (minutes) % Bonus applied to total pay Total payment in USD assuming 100% question correctness

For example, assume you answered 8/12 correctly in 16 minutes and 20 seconds. This means that you will be payed $5.20 + 3*$1.04 = $8.32 based on your correctness and there will be a bonus of 20% added to your total pay. So your total pay would be 1.20 * 8.32 = $9.98.

Note that the above times only apply for answering questions, not including tutorial, questionnaire, or any other miscellaneous portions (so you can take your time on those other portions without affecting your payout).

Once you submit your answer for a question you will get immediate feedback on whether the answer is right or wrong, and the correct answer will be indicated. During the review process the countdown timer is paused. So you can take your time to review the correct answer after submitting your answer to a question.

Anonymity: Your part in this test is anonymous to the requesters. However, because of the nature of web based studies, it is possible that respondents could be identified by the IP address or other electronic record associated with the response. Neither the requester nor anyone involved with this survey will be capturing those data. Any reports or publications based on this test will use only group data and will not identify you or any individual as being affiliated with this project. The only individually-identifying data we receive from Mechanical Turk are your unique identifier and your country. Your data are also stored on Mechanical Turk's servers, and the data there are subject to the Amazon Mechanical Turk Privacy Notice and Participation Agreement.

Your de-Identified Information could be used for future tests without additional informed consent.
